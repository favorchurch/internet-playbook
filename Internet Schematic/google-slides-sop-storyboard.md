# Favor Manila Internet Team — Slide Storyboard & Presenter Script

This document is the visual storyboard and presentation blueprint for the **Favor Manila Internet Team Master SOP**. It is designed to allow easy copy-pasting of text, tables, and structures directly into Google Slides (Standard 4:3 format).

---

## Presentation Design System (Light Mode Theme)

To ensure a premium, modern, and highly legible look, use the following styling tokens when building the deck in Google Slides:

*   **Backgrounds**: 
    *   *Standard Slides*: Clean Off-White (`#FAFAFA`)
    *   *Section Dividers*: Warm Light Grey (`#F5F6F8`)
*   **Typography**:
    *   *Headers*: **Outfit** or **Space Grotesk** (Bold, high tech and clean)
    *   *Body / Tables / Notes*: **Inter** (Excellent readability)
*   **Colors & Accents**:
    *   **Primary Accent (Blue)**: `#1A73E8` (FVR MAIN network, Starlink, status indicators)
    *   **Secondary Accent (Orange)**: `#E65100` (RESI streaming, critical timeline items)
    *   **Alert Accent (Red)**: `#C62828` (Troubleshooting, backups, and dependencies)
*   **Standard (4:3) Visual Layout Structure**: 
    *   **Narrow Canvas Optimization**: Since Standard 4:3 is narrower than Widescreen, avoid cramming more than two columns horizontally.
    *   **Horizontal Flows**: For 3-step or 4-step horizontal sequences, wrap them into a 2x2 grid or stack them vertically to prevent text compression.
    *   **Grid Cards**: Use high-contrast light-grey cards (`#FFFFFF` with `#E0E0E0` borders) for grouping information instead of bullet-point walls.
    *   Highlight key metrics and statuses using solid colored pill-shapes (e.g. `[UNLISTED]` or `[PUBLIC]`).

## Slide 27: Troubleshooting Core Principles
*   **Visual Layout**: Large diagnostic flow block showing bottom-up diagnosis.
*   **Visual Assets**: Step-by-step diagnostic arrow (`#1A73E8`).
*   **Slide Content**:
    *   **Header**: Network Diagnostic Principles
    *   **The Bottom-Up Rule**
        
```
[Power] ──► [Physical Link Lights] ──► [IP Reachability] ──► [App/Platform] ──► [Content]
```
    *   **Core Guidelines**:
        *   **Check Physical First**: Is the cable plugged in? Is the node showing LED light?
        *   **The 60-Second Rule**: When in doubt, restart the device, wait 60 seconds, and re-test.
        *   **Verify SSID**: Ensure the diagnostic laptop is connected to `FVR MAIN` (`10.6.33.x` range).
        *   **Escalation Gate**: If a support volunteer cannot resolve an issue within 5 minutes, escalate directly to the Captain.
*   **Speaker Notes**:
    > "When something goes wrong, don't panic. Follow the Bottom-Up rule. Start with power and physical link lights, then check IP routing, then the apps, and finally the stream content itself. If you can't fix it in 5 minutes, escalate to the Captain so we don't blow our stream windows."

## Slide 28: Playbook A: Starlink Down
*   **Visual Layout**: Horizontal numbered card sequence. Accent alerts in Red.
*   **Visual Assets**: Starlink dish icon, WAN port indicator.
*   **Slide Content**:
    *   **Header**: Playbook A: Starlink Outage
    *   **Symptoms**: No internet on `FVR MAIN`; speedtest = 0; Starlink App shows offline/error.
    *   **Action Steps**:
        1.  **Check Starlink App**: Verify if there is a regional satellite outage notice.
        2.  **Verify Physical Link**: Check the cable from the Starlink dish to the ASUS Rapture WAN 1 port for physical damage.
        3.  **Power Cycle**: Unplug Starlink power, wait 30 seconds, and plug back in. Wait 2 minutes for satellite lock.
        4.  **Confirm Failover**: If Starlink remains offline, confirm that the ASUS router has auto-failed over to PLDT JIREH (WAN 2 active on ASUS admin page at `10.6.33.1`).
        5.  **Test Speed**: Run fast.com wired to confirm failover speed is ≥80 Mbps.
*   **Speaker Notes**:
    > "If our main Starlink dish goes down, we verify the app and physical cables first, then power cycle the dish. Our main ASUS router is configured to automatically failover to our PLDT Jireh router on WAN 2. We just need to check the ASUS admin page to confirm WAN 2 is routing traffic and test the speed."
